Student Affairs

Philosophy — Residence Life

Residential communities as sites of growth, belonging, and ethical practice.

My philosophy of student affairs is rooted in the belief that residential communities are powerful spaces for student growth, belonging, and transformation. I believe students learn not only in classrooms but also through everyday interactions, shared living experiences, and meaningful relationships within residence halls. Because of this, I approach my work in Residence Life with intentional care, presence, and a commitment to supporting students’ holistic development.

Empathy, integrity, and equity

My practice is guided by core values of empathy, integrity, equity, and respect for diverse lived experiences. As an international graduate student working in Residence Life, I regularly engage with students navigating cultural transitions, identity exploration, and personal challenges. These experiences shape how I listen, respond, and design community-building opportunities that help students feel seen and supported. I strive to create residential environments where every student experiences dignity, safety, and a genuine sense of belonging.

Residential life as learning

I view Residence Life as an everyday learning environment where growth happens through conversation, conflict resolution, programming, and reflection. In my role, I support students by fostering inclusive communities, mentoring residents, and responding thoughtfully to crises and concerns. I also collaborate with campus partners to address barriers affecting student well-being, particularly for marginalized and international students. Through this work, I aim to help students develop resilience, responsibility, and confidence in their ability to shape their own paths.

Ethics and aspirations

Ethical responsibility is central to my philosophy. I am committed to maintaining confidentiality, practicing fairness, and making decisions that prioritize student safety and trust. I understand ethical practice as an ongoing process of reflection, accountability, and learning rather than a fixed set of rules. My goal in Residence Life is to cultivate inclusive communities where students are empowered to grow, connect, and thrive—and to contribute to transformative learning experiences within higher education.
