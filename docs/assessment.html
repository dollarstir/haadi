Assessment, evaluation & research

Using evidence to strengthen residential programs and supervision—not only intuition and habit.

Assessment reminds us to ground programs and day-to-day practice in evidence. In Residence Life I gather qualitative signals constantly—hall attendance, informal debriefs after programs, staff check-ins, and documentation in systems such as Maxient, Roompact, or StarRez depending on the workflow. Coursework in student development reinforces pairing those impressions with clearer goals and follow-up questions so we adjust initiatives on purpose.

Artifacts such as my Union Street Center environmental observation and structured RA evaluations show how I document what a community needs and turn that analysis into coaching conversations. My Admissions practicum reinforced how qualitative and quantitative evidence together tell a fuller story about access and readiness for historically marginalized students.

Moving forward, I aim to design programs with explicit outcomes, gather meaningful feedback, and close the loop so residential experiences improve over time—aligned with ACPA/NASPA’s Assessment, Evaluation, and Research competency.
