About me

International graduate student, educator, and residence life practitioner bridging Ghana, IU Bloomington, and Stanford Summer Session.

My name is Abdul Gaffar Haadi. I am an international graduate student from Ghana pursuing an M.S.Ed. in Higher Education and Student Affairs at Indiana University Bloomington (expected May 2026). I hold a Bachelor of Education (Social Studies) from the University of Cape Coast, with a major in Geography and a minor in Economics.

At IU I serve as Assistant Coordinator for Center Management in the Office of Student Life, Residence Life—supervising and mentoring resident assistants and leading a center of seven residential blocks for more than 700 students. I also serve as Graduate Advisor for the Hutton Honors College thematic community at Union Street Center. My graduate internships include the Office of Admissions (Early Outreach and Engagement) and a House Director role with Stanford Summer Session.

Previously, I taught first- through third-year high school students in Ghana through the National Service Scheme—designing engaging lessons, supporting diverse learners, and cultivating inclusive classrooms. Across roles I emphasize residential curriculum, crisis response, conduct-adjacent support, and partnerships that help international and historically marginalized students thrive.

This portfolio shares my Residence Life philosophy, ACPA/NASPA reflections, resume, and artifacts that illustrate supervision, assessment, and community development. Thank you for visiting; please contact me if you would like to connect.
